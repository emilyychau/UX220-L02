The Best Thing I Read

I recently re-read the novel Will Grayson, Will Grayson. It is co-written by John Green and David Levithan. The story is about two teenagers, both named Will Grayson, living in separate cities in the Chicago suburbs of Illinois.

The book is written from the perspective of both Will Graysons; the odd numbered chapters are authored by Green. He writes from the point of view of Will Grayson 1, the first and only, male straight-identifying member of his high school’s Gay-Straight Alliance. The even numbered chapters are authored by Levithan, and are from the point of view of gay Will, also known as Will Grayson 2. The Will Graysons cross paths in the middle of the novel, their stories and interactions slowly become entangled.

There is also a spin-off book that goes in depth about the autobiographical musical written by the character Tiny Cooper. It’s titled Hold Me Closer: The Tiny Cooper Story. Although I have owned this book in paperback for several years, it sits on a bookshelf in my childhood bedroom waiting to be read.
